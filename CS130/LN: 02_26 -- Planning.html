Planning
  • There is an O(N^2) communication overhead in a project with N people.
  • Your planning experience won’t scale.
  • 1-3 person projects are about 3% of all developers
  • 4-10 people teams 10%
  • 11-25 people teams 15%
  • 26-50 people teams 25%
  • 50+ people teams 50%
  • Typical parts of a project plan
    • Team organization
    • Risk analysis
    • Resource requirements (HW, SW, network)
    • Work breakdown (tasks and subtasks, milestones, deliverables)
    • Schedule
    • Reporting mechanism

Risk Management
  • About variance (not mean) of estimated cost
  • Risk estimation
    • What are all the relevant risks 
      • What are the associated probabilities?
      • How big of an impact will the realization of the risk have?
        • Use four-point scale:
          • 4 — negligible risk
          • 3 — marginal
          • 2 — critical risk
          • 1 — catastrophic
        • Ideally the impact would be a cost
    • Risk Assessment in phases:
      • Preliminary — during requirements gathering. Do not know the implementation yet.
      • Lifecycle — during system development
      • Operational — during production
    • Risk Categories
      • Business risk (e.g. market doesn’t exist anymore)
      • Product risk (e.g. Garbage collection unexpectedly stinks)
      • Project risk (e.g., developer leaves)
    • Risk categories can overlap.
    • Fault tree analysis. Risk dependencies and nesting.
    • How to manage risks when they happen?
      • Mitigation — avoid in the first place
      • Monitoring — keep track of warning signals
      • Management — What to do if the risky event happens

Project Estimation
  • One of the hardest parts of software management.
    • Predicting the future
    • Be honest about the limitations of you predictions
  • General ideas:
    • Keep records of previous projects in order to be able to look at similar projects and see how well they went (be careful, these may not be accurate)
    • Use several methods to estimate and do cross checks
    • Assume that something will go wrong (contingency planning)
    • If possible, estimate incrementally.
  • EVA (Earned Value Analysis)
    • BCWS = Budgeted Cost of Work Scheduled
    • BCWP = Budgeted Cost of Work Performed
    • SPI = BCWP / BCWS (Schedule Performance Index)
    • BAC = Budget At Completion (Sum of all BCWS)
    • % done = BCWP / BAC
    • ACWP = Actual Cost of Work Performed 
    • CPI = BCWP / ACWP (Cost Performance Index)
  • Buy vs. Build and project estimation
  • Risk of combinatorial explosion of risk analysis.
  • When you are late:
    • Adding people to a late project makes it later
    • Extended deadline
    • Reduce project scope

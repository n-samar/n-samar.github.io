Sommerville: Chapter 27 — Formal Specification
  • Why are formal methods not used in industry?
    • Successful software engineering. The use of other software engineering methods proved successful.
    • Market changes. Often time-to-market is more relevant than software quality.
    • Limited scope of formal methods. Formal methods are not well suited to specifying user interfaces and user interaction.
    • Limited scalability of formal methods.
  • Organizations that have made the investment in formal methods have reported fewer errors in the delivered software without an increase in development costs.
  • Use of formal methods is increasing in the area of critical systems development, where emergent system properties such as safety, reliability and security are important.
  • Two fundamental approaches to formal specification:
  • An algebraic approach where the system is described in terms of operations and their relationships
  • A model-based approach where a model of the system is built using mathematical constructs such as sets and sequences and the system operations are defined by how they modify the system state.
